Computus Ecclesiasticus

Butcher's
Algorithm

A sophisticated computational method devised to calculate the date of Easter for any year in the Gregorian calendar without the need for tabular references.

Samuel Butcher

Bishop of Meath Author, 'The Ecclesiastical Calendar'

Published in 1877, his work systematized the lunisolar constants into the general solution used today.

Gregorian Precision

Unlike earlier Julian methods, Butcher's algorithm perfectly accounts for the leap year exceptions (years divisible by 100 but not 400), ensuring the liturgical calendar remains in sync with the solar year for millennia.
