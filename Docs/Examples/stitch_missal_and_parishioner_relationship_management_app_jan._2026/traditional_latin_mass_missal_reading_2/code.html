Ordinary of the Mass

[The Priest stands at the foot of the altar.]

In nómine Patris, et Fílii, et Spíritus Sancti. Amen.
In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost. Amen.

Psalm 42:1-5

Introíbo ad altáre Dei.

Ad Deum qui lætíficat juventútem meam.

I will go in to the altar of God.

To God, the joy of my youth.

Júdica me, Deus, et discérne causam meam de gente non sancta: ab hómine iníquo, et dolóso érue me.
Judge me, O God, and distinguish my cause from the nation that is not holy: deliver me from the unjust and deceitful man.

The Canon of the Mass

Mystical Silence Did you know?

The Canon is prayed in a whisper to signify the "cloud of unknowing" that surrounded Moses on Sinai. This silence creates a sacred space where the priest enters the Holy of Holies alone to speak with God face-to-face.

Te ígitur, clementíssime Pater, per Jesum Christum, Fílium tuum, Dóminum nostrum, súpplices rogámus, et pétimus...
We therefore, most humble pray and beseech Thee, most merciful Father, through Jesus Christ, Thy Son, our Lord...

Communion

Hæc commíxtio, et consecrátio Córporis et Sánguinis Dómini nostri Jesu Christi, fiat accipiéntibus nobis in vitam ætérnam. Amen.
May this commingling and consecration of the Body and Blood of our Lord Jesus Christ be to us that receive it effectual to eternal life. Amen.

The Mystery of Commingling

Symbolism

When the priest drops a particle of the Host into the Chalice, it signifies the Reunion of Christ's Body and Blood—symbolizing His glorious Resurrection. Just as death separated His Body from His Blood, this action mystically reunites them before we receive Him.
